Deal Structuring

Structures that get deals done.

The right deal structure is what separates a closed transaction from a failed one. Blackmont works across a range of cash, equity and finance structures to align the interests of buyers and sellers, maximising outcome for both parties.

Structure Type 01
Cash Structures
Cash-based transactions provide sellers with certainty and a clean exit. The proportion of cash at completion depends on the buyer's capitalisation, the presence of institutional debt, and the negotiated terms of settlement. Blackmont structures cash components to maximise what lands in your hands at close.
Structure Type 02
Finance Structures
Finance arrangements including vendor finance, mezzanine debt and earnout components allow buyers to bridge capital gaps while enabling sellers to achieve a stronger headline price. These structures are tailored to the specific risk profile, timeline and appetite of both parties.
Structure Type 03
Equity Structures
Equity rollovers and retained stakes allow sellers to participate in the upside of the business post-acquisition. These structures are particularly common in private equity transactions where management continuity and shared incentive are valued by the incoming buyer.

Most transactions in the $10M to $50M range involve a combination of two or more of these structure types. Blackmont works with both parties to identify the combination that keeps the deal alive, protects the seller's outcome, and gives the buyer a credible path to settlement. Every structure is negotiated on its merits, not applied from a template.
